Akashi, Lady of — daughter of the old recluse of Akashi; courted by Genji during his exile; brought back by him from exile
Akashi, Princess — daughter of Lady of Akashi and Genji
Akikonomu — daughter of Rokujō; Vestal Virgin at Ise; Consort of Emperor Ryōzen
Aoi, Princess — Genji's first wife; her death is recorded at the end of Vol. I
Asagao, Princess — daughter of Prince Momozono; courted by Genji since his boyhood, without success
Ateki — daughter of Tamakatsura's old nurse
Bugo no Suke — brother of Ateki
Chūjō, Lady — daughter of Tō no Chūjō by his legitimate wife; concubine of Emperor Ryōzen
Chūjō no Omoto — maid in service of Higekuro
Chūnagon — maid to Oborozuki
Crown Prince, The — son of Suzaku; afterwards ascends the throne as the New Emperor
Emperor, The Old — Genji's father
Falling Flowers, Lady from the Village of — sister of one of the Old Emperor's court-ladies; under Genji's protection; entrusted with Yūgiri's upbringing
Fujitsubo — the Old Emperor's consort; loved by Genji; sister of Prince Hyōbukyō; aunt of Murasaki
Genji, Prince — son of the Old Emperor by a lady-in-waiting; the hero
Gosechi, Lady — dancer at the winter festival; admired by Genji
Higekuro — brother of Suzaku's consort Lady Jōkyōden; husband of Makibashira; subsequently marries Tamakatsura
Hyōbukyō, Prince — brother of Fujitsubo; father of Murasaki
Iyo no Suke — husband of Utsusemi
Jijū — maid to Suyetsumu
Jōkyōden, Lady — consort of Suzaku
Kaoru — son of Nyosan and Kashiwagi; supposed by the world to be Genji's son
Kashiwagi — eldest son of Tō no Chūjō; in love with Nyosan
Ki no Kami — son of Iyo no Suke by his first wife
Kiritsubo — concubine of the Emperor; Genji's mother
Kōbai — brother of Kashiwagi
Kōkiden — original consort of the Old Emperor; Genji's wicked stepmother; mother of Suzaku
Kojijū — maid to Nyosan
Koremitsu — Genji's favourite retainer
Koremitsu's Daughter — the Gosechi dancer; admired by Yūgiri
Kumoi — younger daughter of Tō no Chūjō; Yūgiri's wife
Left, Minister of the — father of Aoi
Makibashira — Higekuro's mad wife
Moku no Kimi — maid in service of Higekuro
Momozono, Prince — brother of the Old Emperor; father of Princess Asagao
Murasaki — second wife of Genji; daughter of Prince Hyōbukyō; adopted by Genji
Myōbu — a young court lady who introduces Genji to Princess Suyetsumuhana
Niou — son of the New Emperor and the Akashi Princess; Genji's grandson
Nishi no Miya — Murasaki's eldest sister
Nokiba no Ogi — Ki no Kami's sister
Nyosan — favourite daughter of ex-Emperor Suzaku; accepted by Genji as his formal wife; loved by Kashiwagi
Oborozukiyo, Princess — younger sister of Kōkiden; consort of Suzaku; formerly loved by Genji
Ochiba — daughter of Suzaku; married to Kashiwagi; after his death, loved by Yūgiri
Omi, Lady from — illegitimate daughter of Tō no Chūjō; reclaimed by him in error while searching for Tamakatsura
Ōmiya, Princess — mother of Aoi and Tō no Chūjō; sister of the Old Emperor; Yūgiri's maternal grandmother
Ōmyōbu — maid to Fujitsubo
Reikeiden — lady-in-waiting at the Old Emperor's court
Reikeiden, Princess — niece of Kōkiden
Right, Minister of the — father of Kōkiden
Rokujō, Princess — widow of the Old Emperor's brother; loved by Genji in his early days; violently jealous; mother of Akikonomu
Ryōzen, Emperor — reputed son of the Old Emperor, but really son of Genji and Fujitsubo
Saishō — maid to Tamakatsura
Sanjō — Yūgao's maid
Shōni — husband of Tamakatsura's nurse; father of Ateki and Bugo no Suke
Shōnagon — Murasaki's old nurse
Sochi, Prince — Genji's half-brother
Suyetsumuhana (Suyetsumu), Lady — daughter of Prince Hitachi; a timid and eccentric lady with a red nose
Suzaku, Emperor — Genji's half-brother; son of Kōkiden; father of Nyosan and Ochiba
Tamakatsura — daughter of Tō no Chūjō and Yūgao; adopted by Genji
Tayū — swashbuckler in Tsukushi
Tō no Chūjō — Genji's great friend and brother-in-law; brother of Genji's first wife, Aoi
Ukon — Yūgao's maid
Ukon no Jō — faithful retainer to Genji; brother of Ki no Kami
Utsusemi — wife of Iyo no Suke; courted by Genji
Yamato, Governor of — nephew of Ochiba's mother
Yoshikiyo — faithful retainer of Genji; followed him into exile
Yūgao — loved first by Tō no Chūjō, then by Genji; dies in a deserted mansion; mother of Tamakatsura
Yūgiri — Genji's son by Aoi
